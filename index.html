About Me

My primary academic focus lies in nuclear physics and computational physics. I specialize in theoretical methods, nuclear potentials, and reaction mechanisms. My research involves developing and applying nuclear potentials, and I have created folding potentials for systems like 12C + 24Mg and 6He + 12C. I also developed BiFold, a Python code for calculating these potentials.

My interests also extend to nuclear reactions, including medium effects and those involving rare isotopes, and to nuclear astrophysicsstrong>, where I have studied cross sections relevant to nucleosynthesis. In addition, I have contributed to research on photonuclear reactions and analyzing the physical properties of nanocomposites and the optical properties of quantum dots.

For my computational work, I frequently use the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) to solve quantum mechanical equations. To support this work, I developed AIMpy, a dedicated Python code for solving Schr√∂dinger-like equations.
